Project AI

Operation All-In (Op. AI) is a first-person shooter set against a number of 'dumb' assault drones and a 'smart' commander drone. Alone, the assault drones can be easily dispatched by taking careful consideration of their camera view. However, when paired with a smart, radar equipped commander, they become a responsive threat that can be hard to predict.

This project ​focused on AI techniques, using a custom made finite state machine and fuzzy logic for the AI opponents. Furthermore, one of my goals was to not use any external assets, thus leading to the simplistic art style used in the prototype as all assets were created within Unity.

Created in the Unity Engine using C#.

Perlin Noise Procedural Terrain

This is my procedural terrain generator in OpenGL, C++. It generates a terrain using either the Diamond-Step (Square-step) algorithm, or Perlin noise. It includes 3D camera controls controls with WASD and the mouse, the terrain can be regenerated during runtime, and the water and clouds have their own separate animations. All of the objects in the scene have diffuse lighting, and the terrain has multiple textures which are blended depending on the height of the terrain.  

Programmed in Visual Studio using C++, with OpenGL for graphics rendering.

C++ Raytracer

This is a ray caster program using C++ and object-oriented programming techniques. It uses phong shading to calculate ambient, specular and diffuse intensities for each object, with hard shadows cast on both the ground and other objects based on the position and colour of nearby light sources.

Programmed in Visual Studio using C++, with OpenGL for graphics rendering.

Art Projects

I was tasked with creating several artistic projects consisting of animations, models and materials.

The first was a 'marble run' animation, focused on creating a 'physically accurate' animation whilst employing some of the 12 principles of animation. This was keyframed entirely by hand and rendered in Eevee.

The second was a high-poly vehicle model, based on a BMW. I used a side and top profile as references, and a mirror and subdivision modifier to save time and increase the level of detail in the model Furthermore, the model was rigged to allow for steering and automatic wheel movement, ready to be animated within Blender.

The third and final project involved creating a render that imitates a given real life photograph. I used physically based rendering (PBR) materials created in Substance Designer and the cycles renderer to achieve realistic lighting and reflections within my scene.

Created in Blender, using Substance Designer for textures.

Tides of War

Tides of War is a wave-based arcade flight game. The player is thrust into the middle of a war, fighting as the last defender of their island. Use an experimental flight suit to push back the attackers until reinforcements arrive and save your island.

This prototype was created as part of a game conceptualisation coursework, and only needed a trailer, however I created a fully working prototype including AI opponents that fought back and the ability to eliminate them with both a cannon and missiles.

The Asylum

The Asylum was a virtual reality horror puzzle game, created as part of a group project. My segment of the game included an experience rather than a puzzle, where the level changes around the player when they are not looking. Walls disappear, rooms change and doors close all outside of the player's view, creating an unnerving but interesting experience, especially when viewed in virtual reality. This was also my first introduction to Blender and Unity in 3D.

Modelled and textured in Blender, created in the Unity Engine in C#.
